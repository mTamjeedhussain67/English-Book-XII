### Connection to Unit Theme: Self-Discipline
The myth of Icarus serves as a powerful allegory for the importance of self-discipline:
- **Daedalus** represents wisdom, planning, and restraint.
- **Icarus** represents youthful impulsiveness and lack of self-control.
- The story illustrates how ignoring disciplined guidance leads to tragic consequences.

## 📝 STORY SUMMARY (Section-by-Section)

### Part 1: Daedalus – The Master Architect
Daedalus was among the most cunning mortals who "learned the secrets of the gods." He built the wonderful Labyrinth for King Minos of Crete. However, when the king's favor changed, Daedalus was imprisoned in a tower with his young son, Icarus.

### Part 2: The Plan for Escape
Escape by sea was impossible as every ship was guarded. Watching sea-gulls—"the only creatures that were sure of liberty"—Daedalus conceived a plan. He gathered feathers, fastened them with thread, molded them with wax, and fashioned two great wings. After practice, he learned to fly: "by waving his arms he could winnow the air and cleave it."

### Part 3: The Father's Warning
Daedalus made wings for Icarus and taught him carefully. He gave a critical warning: **"Remember, never to fly very low or very high, for the fogs about the earth would weigh you down, but the blaze of the sun will surely melt your feathers apart if you go too near."** For Icarus, "these cautions went in at one ear and out by the other."

### Part 4: The Flight – Joy and Recklessness
On the day of escape, they rose from Crete. At first, there was "terror in the joy." But as Icarus felt himself sustained, "he forgot everything in the world but joy." He forgot his father's warnings and "stretched out his arms to the sky and made towards the highest heavens."

### Part 5: The Tragic Fall
As Icarus flew higher, the air grew warmer. The heat of the sun melted the wax; feathers fell "like snowflakes." Icarus fell "like a leaf tossed down the wind" and drowned in the sea.

### Part 6: Aftermath and Memorial
Daedalus named the nearest island Icaria in memory of his son. In heavy grief, he hung up his wings as an offering in the temple of Apollo in Sicily. "Never again did he attempt to fly."

## ❓ COMPREHENSION QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Short Answer Questions

**Q1: How does the story reflect the dangers of unchecked ambition?**
**A:** The story shows that ambition without discipline leads to destruction. Icarus, overcome by joy, ignores his father's warnings and flies toward "the highest heavens." His unchecked desire for freedom causes him to fly too close to the sun, melting the wax in his wings and leading to his death.

**Q2: Compare and contrast Daedalus and Icarus.**

| Daedalus | Icarus |
| :--- | :--- |
| Cunning, wise, experienced | Young, impulsive, inexperienced |
| Plans carefully; teaches "carefully" | Forgets warnings; "cautions went in at one ear..." |
| Values discipline and caution | Values joy and freedom above safety |
| Grieves deeply after loss | Suffers consequences of recklessness |

**Q3: What is the significance of Daedalus dedicating his wings to Apollo after Icarus' fall?**
**A:** Hanging his wings in Apollo's temple signifies: (1) gratitude for survival; (2) renunciation of dangerous ambition—"Never again did he attempt to fly"; (3) memorial to his son—an offering acknowledging human limits before divine power.

**Q4: What moral lessons can be drawn from the story? Provide examples from the text.**
**A:** (1) Heed wise counsel: Daedalus warned Icarus to avoid extremes; ignoring this led to disaster. (2) Balance ambition with discipline: Icarus lacked the self-discipline to stay within safe limits. (3) Joy without caution is dangerous: Icarus's excitement overrode his reason.

**Q5: Did Daedalus foresee Icarus' fall when he warned him about the sun? Justify.**
**A:** Yes, Daedalus clearly anticipated the risk. His specific warning—"the blaze of the sun will surely melt your feathers apart if you go too near"—shows he understood the physical danger. The word "surely" indicates certainty, and he taught Icarus "carefully," suggesting deep concern.

## ✍️ TRUE/FALSE QUESTIONS

| Statement | Answer | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1. Daedalus built the Labyrinth for King Minos. | ✅ **True** | "He once built... a wonderful Labyrinth". |
| 2. Daedalus and Icarus escaped Crete using a boat. | ❌ **False** | They escaped using wings made of feathers and wax. |
| 3. Icarus followed his father's advice carefully. | ❌ **False** | "Cautions went in at one ear and out by the other". |
| 4. The wax in Icarus' wings melted near the sun. | ✅ **True** | "The heat of the sun had melted the wax...". |
| 5. Daedalus named a nearby island after his son. | ✅ **True** | "The nearest island he named Icaria...". |
| 6. Icarus' fall was caused by strong winds. | ❌ **False** | Caused by sun's heat melting wax, not wind. |
| 7. Daedalus continued to experiment with flying. | ❌ **False** | "Never again did he attempt to fly". |
| 8. Daedalus made an offering to Apollo. | ✅ **True** | "He hung up his wings as an offering". |
